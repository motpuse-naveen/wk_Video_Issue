Protein S Deficiency

Protein S is another natural anticoagulant normally produced by the liver. APC requires protein S to inactivate certain clotting factors. When the level of protein S is deficient, this inactivation process is diminished and the risk of thrombosis increases. As with protein C deficiency, patients with protein S deficiency have a greater risk for recurrent venous thrombosis early in life, including risk for PE (Bunn & Bauer, 2017).

Thromboses frequently occur in axillary, mesenteric, and cerebral veins. Warfarin-induced skin necrosis is also possible with protein S deficiency. A number of conditions may lead to acquired protein S deficiency including pregnancy, DIC, liver disease, nephritic syndrome, HIV infection, and the use of L-asparaginase.
