Purpose

The choice of an IV solution depends on the purpose of its administration. Generally, IV fluids are given to achieve one or more of the following goals:

  • To provide water, electrolytes, and nutrients to meet daily requirements
  • To replace water and correct electrolyte deficits
  • To administer medications and blood products

IV solutions contain dextrose and/or electrolytes mixed in various proportions with water. Pure, electrolyte-free water can never be given by IV because it rapidly enters RBCs and causes them to rupture (Sterns, 2017b).
