LYMPHOMA

The lymphomas are neoplasms of cells of lymphoid origin. These tumors usually start in lymph nodes but can involve lymphoid tissue in the spleen, GI tract (e.g., the wall of the stomach), liver, or bone marrow (see Chapter 31, Fig. 31-1). They are often classified according to the degree of cell differentiation and the origin of the predominant malignant cell. Lymphomas are broadly classified into two categories: Hodgkin lymphoma and NHL.
